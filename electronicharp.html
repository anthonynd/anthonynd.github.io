Electronic Harp

May 2015

Back to Homepage

The electronic light harp was a final group project for my Digital Electronics course in CEGEP. Each group had an Arduino board to work with and the goal of the project was to make anything we'd like.

Inspired by Youtube videos like this.

Tools used
  • the Arduino board
  • several red light diodes
  • a couple of wooden planks (for the frame)
  • various colored wires
  • a pair of wirestrippers
  • a soldering iron

The electronic light harp consists of 9 light diodes planted in a wooden frame. 8 of them play the notes of an octave while the 9th toggles between play and song mode. In play mode, the user plays their own notes and in song mode, each of the 8 light diodes plays a pre-programmed song.

This was programmed in the Arduino language (a variant of C).
